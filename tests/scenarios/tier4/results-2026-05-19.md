## What this confirms

Tier 3 sub-agents share the parent session's context. Tier 4 spawns a
brand-new Claude Code session in an isolated HOME — so every signal
that fires here is praxis-ai working from a clean state, not me
performing the praxis behaviour on its behalf.

In particular:
- The F0 classifier protocol (TRIVIAL vs NON-TRIVIAL) survives the
  cold-start. The model loads `~/.praxis/phase-flow.md` via the
  CLAUDE.md `@-import` chain and applies the classification.
- The AST hook fires for a real LLM-driven Bash call (TR3), not just
  for a synthetic payload sent by `praxis doctor --verify`.
- Skill auto-discovery resolves the 6 lifted skill manifests from a
  fresh `~/.claude/skills/` directory.

## Cost

5 scenarios × Haiku 4.5 ≈ $0.05–0.10 total per full run. The runner
is opt-in via `npm run test:tier4`; it does NOT execute during
`npm test` or in CI.
